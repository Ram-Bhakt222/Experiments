## 1. Core Thesis — Why HALO Wins

HALO's category is "consumer AI apps where the magic moment is a photo-realistic personal transformation." The moat in this category is **momentum + distribution speed**, not technical novelty — OpenAI or FAL could ship a competing primitive overnight. So the game is:

1. **Magic moment first.** Make the "your face stays locked, only the hair changes" experience the most replicated reaction shot in the category. The product literally IS the hook.
2. **Distribution is the scarce resource.** Spend the first 90 days converting product moments into shareable video assets — not on more features.
3. **Creative is the targeting.** Don't run "beauty interest" Meta ads. Run 30 different creative variants and let the engagement signal sort the audience.
4. **Relationships, not attention.** Build an in-house creator collective (curly, gray, balding, brides, mothers, kids, men's fade) — own your distribution rather than rent it from agencies.

## 2. The Magic Moment (HALO's "Aha")

Cal AI's magic moment is "take a photo of your food and get calories." Landed AI's is "AI mock-interview." Cluely's is "AI helps me cheat in real time."

**HALO's magic moment:** *Same face. Same outfit. Same background. Twelve haircuts and a color season — in 60 seconds.*

That single shot — the face-preserving image edit — IS the hook. Every piece of content, every ad creative, every influencer brief should center it. If a video isn't built around that reveal, it isn't HALO content.

### The Reveal Loop
1. **Establishing shot** — creator in current hair (0–3 s)
2. **The setup line** — "I let AI tell me every haircut I'd actually look good in" (3–6 s)
3. **The grid reveal** — 4–8 styles flash by on the same face (6–11 s)
4. **The "wait, this one" moment** — pause on the surprise winner (11–14 s)
5. **CTA** — "Link in bio" or "Comment HAIR" (14–15 s)

Every hook variant must hit (1)→(4) within 15 seconds. The "see it move" video feature *is* the share asset.

## 3. The 6–15 Second Viral Formula — Hook Library

The Cal AI / Landed AI rule: **product must appear within the first 15 seconds.** For HALO, the product needs to appear within 5 — the face-preservation reveal IS what stops the scroll.

### Tier-1 Hooks (build first)
1. **POV** — "POV: I let an AI try every haircut on me before I commit"
2. **Reverse reveal** — Start with the winning AI cut, then "this is what I actually look like"
3. **Stylist roulette** — "I'm showing my hairstylist these and letting her pick"
4. **Color season exposé** — "AI told me I've been wearing the wrong colors for 10 years"
5. **The 'avoid' list** — "AI showed me what I'd look like with all my dream haircuts. Three are crimes."
6. **Decade test** — "Me at 20, 30, 40, 50 — but the hair, not the face"
7. **Mom/daughter / siblings** — same face, different hair, two generations
8. **Hair loss POV** — "I'm losing my hair. Here's every cut that would actually work."
9. **Salt-and-pepper** — "Going gray on purpose. AI showed me every version."
10. **Bride** — "12 wedding hair options without leaving the couch"

### Tier-2 Hooks (after tier-1 proves out)
- "What my hair says about me" — read-my-hair format
- "Why my stylist won't let me cut this" — reveal "forbidden" cuts
- "Color season therapy" — emotional/identity angle
- "Hair therapy with AI" — quiet, intimate, ASMR-coded
- "AI roasted my haircut" — self-deprecating, Gen Z native

### Sound + Aesthetic Codes
Lean into TikTok native sounds, Pinterest aesthetic codes, and adjacent communities:
- Clean girl / mob wife / coastal grandmother / coquette
- Curly hair community (#curlygirlmethod)
- 4c/4b natural hair community (this is a HUGE underserved AI demographic — most beauty AI is white-coded; HALO must prove it works on all textures *visibly*)
- Men's fade / barbershop content
- Hair-loss / hims / hers / regrowth crowd
- Bridal / wedding planning

## 4. The Micro-Influencer + UGC Playbook

The MYN notebook flagged this as not-applicable. For HALO it's the **primary growth engine.**

### Recruitment
- **The "paid promo?" DM trick.** Open the cold DM with literally the words "paid promo?" — optimizes for inbox-preview open rate. The notebook cites this as a high-ROI move.
- **Pool size:** 50 micro-influencers (1K–10K followers) in the first 90 days. Engagement at that tier averages 5.7% vs. 1.7% for >100K creators.
- **Niche pools (recruit ~6 from each):**
  - Curly + texture diversity (3a–4c)
  - Salt-and-pepper / going-gray
  - Hair loss / thinning / men's hims-adjacent
  - Bridal / wedding
  - Men's barbershop / fade
  - Career-pivot reinvention (women 30–45)
  - Kids' hair (parent accounts)
  - Color-season / personal-styling

### Compensation
- **Bundle, don't pay-per-video.** Pay $400–$800 upfront for a bundle of 4 videos. Lowers effective CPM and gives the creator room to test hooks.
- **Performance bonus:** add a $200 bump on any video that crosses 100K views.
- **No exclusivity** in the first cohort — let creators post for competitors. The viral asset matters more than the lockup.

### Brief Template (give every creator the same)
1. The product must appear in the first 5 seconds.
2. The "same face, different hair" reveal must happen by second 11.
3. The "see it move" hair-motion clip is the closer.
4. The creator's voiceover is the *only* required script element — no scripted brand copy.
5. No filter on skin or face — the magic is that the face is untouched. Filters undercut the proof.
6. Hashtag: `#HALOhair` `#tryeverycut` + creator's normal stack.

## 5. The Cal AI / Landed AI / Cluely Adaptation

The category-defining pattern from the notebook:
- Team photo as a viral asset
- 6–15 second video format with product in frame fast
- Visionary + Integrator pairing
- Radical transparency + controlled controversy

### HALO's version of the team photo
The Cluely playbook ("world-class engineers under 25 in matching hoodies at the center of the tech universe") would *fail* for healthcare-adjacent MYN. For HALO it can work if adapted — the right framing is **"the AI builder + the master stylist"**:

- **Ram** — technical founder, the AI/build side, on-screen credible
- **A real credentialed stylist or colorist co-collaborator** — the human authority that says "yes, this is what I'd actually recommend"
- Optional third: a celebrity-adjacent / aesthetic-coded face (Gen Z attractor)

That trio shot — same energy as Cluely's team photo, but the credibility frame is "AI + craftsman" rather than "AI + ourselves" — is more shareable than either alone. **Action: book a half-day photo + video shoot in the first 30 days.**

### Visionary + Integrator
- **Visionary:** Ram (product + AI)
- **Integrator gap:** if HALO has no operator running the daily growth bus, that is the #1 hire / contract role to fill. A part-time growth lead from the Cal AI / Landed AI playbook adjacency is realistic.

### Controlled controversy
HALO's safest stunt material:
- Show celebrities' AI haircut alternatives (transformative use, not deceptive — fair-use friendly if framed editorially)
- Publish your AI haircuts on the team accounts publicly and let the comments roast you
- "Worst AI haircut I generated this week" series

Avoid:
- Faking before/afters
- Pretending an AI-generated face is a real testimonial
- Scalp-condition / hair-loss "diagnosis" claims (FDA territory)

## 6. Meta + TikTok Ads — Creative-Led

Targeting is dead. **Creative IS the targeting.** Run 20–30 creatives, let CTR + watch-time + saves sort the audience.

### Meta Ads — first month
- 30 creatives across 6 personas (curly, gray, men's, bridal, mom, hair-loss)
- Each creative = single hook from § 3
- Stop the scroll in < 3 s. The face-locked reveal is the pattern interrupt.
- "Green-screen walkthrough" format: creator overlaid on the HALO web UI, narrating the report
- $50/day per ad-set, kill anything under 1.5% CTR after 48 h
- Target metric: CAC < $4–8 per install (consumer-AI app benchmark)

### TikTok — organic + paid
- **Daily posting** is non-negotiable (the algorithm trains on cadence)
- **In-house creator Discord/Slack** — build it in the first 30 days; don't outsource creator management to a general agency
- TikTok Spark Ads on the top 3 organic videos every week — boost what's already proven

### Pinterest — under-allocated channel
Pinterest is *the* hair-search channel. Most consumer-AI apps ignore it. HALO should:
- Post all 12 styles from every demo preset as separate pins
- Tag aggressively by aesthetic code (clean girl, mob wife, etc.)
- This is the cheapest long-tail SEO surface in the category

## 9. Anti-Patterns (DO NOT)

- **Fake AI-avatar testimonials.** Even in consumer-AI this destroys trust faster than it scales.
- **Filters on faces in marketing.** The whole HALO promise is that your face is *untouched*. A beauty filter in an ad nukes the proof.
- **Scalp-health / hair-loss diagnosis claims.** Stay clearly cosmetic. FDA/medical-device territory is not where a consumer hair app survives.
- **Single-ethnicity, single-texture marketing reel.** Most beauty AI is white-coded. HALO must visibly work on 4c/4b textures, gray hair, alopecia, etc., in the *first* round of creatives — not as a later inclusion afterthought.
- **Outsource creator management to a generalist agency.** Build the in-house Discord.
- **Spend on paid before organic proves the hook.** Get 3–5 organic videos past 100K views before scaling paid. The notebook is explicit: the creative wins or loses the ad, not the targeting.
- **Targeting before creative.** Let the creative sort the audience.
- **"Salesy fluff" without proof.** Every transformation claim needs the side-by-side photo. The product is the proof.
